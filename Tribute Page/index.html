Marie Curie the scientist

Marie Curie Timeline

  • 1867 - Born Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1891 - Went to Paris to study at Sorbonne University where she read physics and mathematics.
  • 1895 - Married Pierre Curie. She adopted the French spelling of her name – Marie.
  • 1902 - Isolated radium (as radium chloride), determining its atomic weight as 225.93.
  • 1903 - Marie and Pierre were awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics jointly with Henri Becquerel for their combined, though separate, work on radioactivity. Furthermore, she passed her doctorate thesis in Physics.
  • 1906 - Pierre dies in a traffic accident on April 19. Marie takes over his professorship at the Sorbonne in May.
  • 1910 - Marie’s fundamental treatise on radioactivity is published.
  • 1911 - Won her second Nobel Prize, this time in chemistry for creating a means of measuring radioactivity.
  • 1914 - WWI begins in 1914. Throughout the war Marie, with the help of her daughter Irène devotes herself to making X-ray technology available for use in the medical treatment of wounded soldiers.
  • 1922 - Focused her research on the chemistry of radioactive substances and the medical applications of these substances.
  • 1932 - Helped open the Radium Institute in Warsaw. Her sister, Bronislawa, is named director of the institute.
  • 1934 - Died near Sallanches, France. Her death is the result of leukemia caused by exposure to radiation.
  • 1944 - American chemists discover a new element. They name it curium in honor of Marie and Pierre Curie.
